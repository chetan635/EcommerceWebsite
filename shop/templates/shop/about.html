My Awesome Cart

My Awesome Cart was founded in October 2007 by Sachin Bansal and Binny Bansal, who were both alumni of the Indian Institute of Technology Delhi and formerly worked for Amazon.[14][15][16] The company initially focused on online book sales with country-wide shipping. Following its launch, Flipkart slowly grew in prominence; by 2008, it was receiving 100 orders per day.[17] In 2010, Flipkart acquired the Bangalore-based social book discovery service WeRead from Lulu.com.[18] In late 2011, Flipkart made several acquisitions relating to digital distribution, including Mime360.com[19] and the digital content library of Bollywood portal Chakpak.[20] In February 2012, the company unveiled its DRM-free online music store Flyte. However, the service was unsuccessful due to competition from free streaming sites, and shut down in June 2013.[21][22][23][24] In May 2012, Flipkart acquired Letsbuy, an online electronics retailer.[25] In May 2014, Flipkart acquired Myntra, an online fashion retailer, for â‚¹20 billion (US$280 million).[26] Myntra continues to operate alongside Flipkart as a standalone subsidiary; the site focuses on an upscale, "fashion-conscious" market, while Flipkart itself focuses on the mainstream market and major international brands.[27].
